# 00_RESEARCH.md — What Actually Wins GTA Cleaning Clients

> Built from research on real Toronto/GTA cleaning company sites + 2026
> conversion data (June 2026). Every design decision in the other docs traces
> back to a finding here. This is WHY, not just what.

## Who hires a web developer in this niche (and who doesn't)
- **Hireable:** independent / family-owned GTA cleaning businesses (1–15
  staff) with a weak or dated site, or just a Google Business listing + a
  phone number. They KNOW they're losing after-hours leads to competitors who
  let people book online. This is the sweet spot.
- **Not worth chasing:** national franchises (Molly Maid, Merry Maids) — they
  have corporate sites; and pure-marketplace players (Handy, Jobber-run shops)
  — they don't own their funnel. Target the independent operator who owns a
  domain and answers their own phone.
- **The pain you're selling against:** "Phone calls get missed: one missed
  call can mean one lost recurring client. After-hours leads are common." A
  site that captures leads 24/7 is the product.

## The #1 finding: pricing transparency converts, "call for a quote" doesn't
- The fastest-converting indie sites show **numbers upfront** — competitor
  comparisons, instant pricing, quotes-before-commit, **price from room count
  alone**. "Transparency builds trust faster than 'call for a quote' ever
  will."
- TAKEAWAY → the demo's hero feature is an **instant price calculator** that
  estimates from bedrooms / bathrooms / service type / frequency. This is the
  thing that makes the demo read as real $1k-tier work instead of a brochure.
  If we build ONE impressive thing, it's this.

## The dual-CTA hero (steal this exactly)
- Best sites put TWO CTAs above the fold: one for **ready buyers** ("Book
  Now") and one for **comparison shoppers** ("Calculate Your Price"). Captures
  both buying stages. We do this.

## Header rules (non-negotiable, every converting site does it)
- **Phone number + booking CTA in the header, on every page.** Always visible.
- Strong sites offer **3+ ways to convert on every page**: phone, booking
  button, quote/calculator, (optional chat). We'll do phone + book + calculator.

## GTA-specific trust signals (these recur on every top Toronto site)
Show these prominently — they are table stakes in this market:
- **Insured & bonded**
- **Eco-friendly / non-toxic products** (huge in Toronto — families, allergies)
- **Background-checked / security-screened cleaners**
- **100% satisfaction guarantee** (re-clean if not happy)
- **Local & family-owned** + a founder story (indie's edge over franchises)
- **Google reviews with real attribution + dates** (not hand-picked quotes)
- **Service-area / neighbourhood list**: North York, Scarborough, Etobicoke,
  Mississauga, Vaughan, Markham, Richmond Hill, Brampton, Downtown. Toronto
  sites lean hard on local-area coverage; listing neighbourhoods reads local
  and helps SEO.

## Services to feature (the standard GTA menu)
Residential is the core. The recurring menu across top GTA sites:
- Recurring home cleaning (weekly / bi-weekly / monthly) ← the revenue engine
- One-time deep clean
- Move-in / move-out (end of tenancy — "get your deposit back")
- Condo / apartment cleaning
- Post-construction / post-reno
- Office / commercial (secondary)
- Airbnb / short-term turnover (a real growing segment in Toronto)

## Real GTA pricing reality (so the demo's numbers look credible)
- Market range: ~$150 for a condo/small home up to ~$600 for a large multi-
  bed/bath home. Most price by home size or by estimated hours.
- Recurring plans get **5–20% discounts** by frequency — show this; it drives
  the highest-value (recurring) bookings.
- Our demo calculator should land inside this real band, not invent numbers.

## Conversion features ranked (what to actually build vs skip)
1. Instant price calculator (room-count based) — highest impact. BUILD.
2. Dual hero CTA (book / calculate) — BUILD.
3. Sticky header with phone + Book Now — BUILD.
4. Real-looking Google-style reviews w/ name + neighbourhood + date — BUILD.
5. Trust badge row (insured / eco / guarantee / screened) — BUILD.
6. Service-area neighbourhood list — BUILD.
7. Recurring-frequency discount toggle in calculator — BUILD (drives recurring).
8. Founder/“local family-owned” story block — BUILD (indie edge).
9. Real booking software (Zenbooker/Launch27/Jobber) — DO NOT build; that's
   paid SaaS the real client integrates later. For the DEMO, all booking CTAs
   and the calculator's final "Book" action open Shubh's Instagram DM. A note
   in the footer can say "booking system integration available."

## How this maps to selling the demo
When Shubh shows this to the Discord network or a real GTA lead, the pitch is:
"Most cleaning sites here still say 'call for a quote' and lose after-hours
leads. This one prices the job instantly and captures the booking 24/7 —
that's the difference between a brochure and a site that books jobs." The
calculator is the proof. That's what justifies $300–$1k.

## What to consciously AVOID (lessons from the portfolio build)
- No dark/space theme. Cleaning = light, fresh, clean, trustworthy.
- No 3D, no particle cursor, no galaxy. This is a conversion tool, not art.
- No fake stats that can be disproven; reviews are clearly a demo.
- Don't bury the phone or the price. Don't make anyone "call for a quote."
